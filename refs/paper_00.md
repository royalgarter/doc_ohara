# **The OHARA Space-Time Graph Architecture**

---

## **1. Introduction**

- **1.1 The Scalability Impasse in RAG** - Modern RAG variants sound powerful on paper: GraphRAG builds community clusters, PageIndex exploits document structure via TOC-guided tree descent, LLM Wiki incrementally grows a semantic network. Yet each breaks down when data explodes. Flat-chunk RAG suffers "lost in the middle" (Liu et al., 2024). GraphRAG pays linear-in-chunks LLM extraction cost plus super-linear community construction, and its community detection is at risk of degrading as entity overlap saturates across thousands of documents. PageIndex is single-document - it has no cross-document reasoning at all, so scaling beyond one PDF means N independent trees with no connections. LLM Wiki lacks structural document hierarchy (Markdown files with wiki links, no DoCO types), so documents with complex internal structure (textbooks, legal contracts, technical standards) flatten into an undifferentiated graph soup. Each system optimizes one dimension; none makes time, ontology, and document structure simultaneously first-class as corpora grow.
- **1.2 The OHARA Thesis** - A knowledge base that is exploding in size demands a *spatial*, *topical*, and *temporal* mental model - not a longer flat list, not a 2D node-link diagram that becomes hairballs, but a **Space-Time Graph** where time flows along a navigable tunnel axis, an ontology sunburst organizes the cross-section into stable topic columns, and document structure unfolds as radial discs at each temporal position. This metaphor is designed to turn "more data" from a liability into a visual advantage: more documents fill more disc positions and densify topic columns, making cross-document patterns *more* visible, not less. We state this as the design bet the architecture is built around; its human-facing form is an open hypothesis (H3, Section 8.5) with the study protocol prepared, not a demonstrated result.
- **1.3 Contributions** - (a) Space-Time Graph data model unifying structural hierarchy (DoCO), temporal decay classes, and cross-document entity/SUMO pivots; (b) Multi-phase hybrid retrieval engine with explainable tier classification, whose measured value (companion evaluation) is **tiered provenance at ranking parity with the best single signal, plus corroboration-gated abstention on unanswerable queries (45.6% vs. 0.0% for top-k at default weights; tuning trades abstention to 31.2% for ranking parity)** — not ranking superiority; (c) 3D sunburst-tunnel visualization mapping time → Z-axis, ontology → sunburst cross-section, document structure → concentric disc rings, decay class → temporal-reach auras; (d) SUMO-ontology-grounded semantic validation (22,700-entry index); (e) Unified architecture that subsumes 20+ RAG patterns (Standard, Corrective, Self, Speculative, REFEED, Agentic, CoR, Reasoning, Conversational, Adaptive, Fusion, Hybrid, Context-Aware, Citation-Aware, Hierarchical, Memory-Augmented, Multi-Hop, Prompt-Augmented, Context-Ranking, Entity-Pivot) as configuration toggles on one engine.
- **1.4 Paper Organization** - Roadmap of §2–§9.

The scaling critique is grounded as follows. GraphRAG's indexing pass extracts entities and relations from every chunk with an LLM and then builds and summarizes hierarchical communities (Edge et al., 2024) — a cost structure that subsequent work identifies as its main deployment barrier: LightRAG's evaluation (Guo et al., 2024) motivates its own design by GraphRAG's high token consumption and expensive incremental updates (any corpus change re-triggers community construction). For calibration, OHARA's full semantic-graph ingest measures ~$12 per 1,000 documents on `gemini-2.5-flash-lite` (Section 5) with content-hash idempotent re-ingest — incremental by construction. PageIndex's limitation is architectural, not empirical: its index is one reasoning tree per document, so a 100-document corpus is 100 disconnected trees with no cross-document edge of any kind — cross-document questions are outside its representational capacity, not merely unbenchmarked. LLM Wiki's flattening is likewise structural: its nodes are wiki-style pages linked by semantic association, with no DoCO typing, so a 300-page standard and a 3-paragraph memo produce the same node granularity — within-document navigation has no substrate. The quantitative corpus-size sweep (retrieval quality at 10/100/1,000+ docs per system) remains open and is stated as hypothesis H4 (Section 8.5).

> **Citation**: Darren Edge, Ha Trinh, Newman Cheng, Joshua Bradley, Alex Chao, Apurva Mody, Steven Truitt, and Jonathan Larson. 2024. *From Local to Global: A Graph RAG Approach to Query-Focused Summarization*. arXiv:2404.16130.
> **Citation**: Zirui Guo, Lianghao Xia, Yanhua Yu, Tu Ao, and Chao Huang. 2024. *LightRAG: Simple and Fast Retrieval-Augmented Generation*. arXiv:2410.05779.

## **2. Why Existing RAG Architectures Break at Scale**

- **2.1 Flat-Chunk RAG: The List Without a Map** - The retrieved list stays top-k, but what stands behind each rank degrades as the corpus grows: candidate pools multiply, and lexical signals collide across documents. We have one measured instance of this: on a 229-document corpus, BM25 inverts from strongest retrieval signal *within* one document to weakest *across* documents, as common phrasing collides corpus-wide (companion evaluation, §7.1.1 comparability note). And regardless of ranking quality, no spatial or temporal model helps the user understand *where* retrieved knowledge sits relative to other knowledge. You get ranked text, not a map.
- **2.2 GraphRAG / Community-Based: The Entity Saturation Risk** - GraphRAG's power is cross-document entity linking. But entity extraction cost is O(n × chunks_per_doc) in LLM calls, community construction on top of it is super-linear, and as corpora grow, entity overlap saturates - "Bitcoin" appears in 200 documents, creating a star topology that risks collapsing community detection into one giant cluster. The graph tends toward a hairball. No temporal dimension means you cannot see *when* documents were written, only that they share entities. (Cost structure cited from the systems' own papers, §1; the saturation curve itself is hypothesis H4.)
- **2.3 PageIndex: Structure Without Connection** - PageIndex achieves 98.7% on FinanceBench by exploiting within-document TOC hierarchy. But it is fundamentally single-document: no `SIMILAR_TO` edges, no entity pivot, no SUMO tags, no temporal scoring, no multi-phase fusion, no tier system, no feedback loop. Scaling to 100 documents means 100 independent trees with zero cross-pollination. A user asking "how do these three contracts differ?" gets nothing from PageIndex - it has no inter-document graph.
- **2.4 LLM Wiki: Semantics Without Structure** - LLM Wiki builds an elegant persistent semantic network with Louvain community detection, Adamic-Adar weighting, and "surprising connections." But it discards document structure entirely - no DoCO types (Chapter/Section/Paragraph/Table), no structural traversal, no TOC-level navigation. A 300-page technical standard becomes a bag of wiki links. The graph handles cross-document semantics well but cannot guide a user *within* a document's architecture.
- **2.5 The Dimension Gap** - Summary table showing what each system lacks:

  | Capability | Flat RAG | GraphRAG | PageIndex | LLM Wiki | **OHARA** |
  |---|---|---|---|---|---|
  | Structural hierarchy (DoCO) | - | - | ✓ | - | **✓** |
  | Cross-document graph | - | ✓ | - | ✓ | **✓** |
  | Temporal decay model | - | - | - | - | **✓** |
  | Ontology-grounded tags | - | - | - | - | **✓** |
  | Spatial visualization | - | - | - | 2D graph | **3D sunburst-tunnel** |
  | Multi-phase fusion | - | partial | - | 4-signal | **8-signal** |
  | Tier explainability | - | - | - | - | **3-tier** |

  **Key observation**: No prior system combines structural hierarchy + cross-document graph + temporal model + spatial visualization. The gap is the missing *spatiotemporal* dimension in knowledge base navigation.

**Open (→ H4, Section 8.5)**: this section's degradation arguments are architectural, supported by the cited systems' own papers, but not yet measured head-to-head by us. The quantitative form — entity-extraction cost vs. corpus size for GraphRAG, recall vs. corpus size for flat RAG, and a PageIndex multi-document adaptation if one emerges — is declared as hypothesis H4 rather than asserted. One in-house data point already exists: on a 229-document corpus, corpus-wide BM25 falls from strongest signal (within one document) to weakest (across documents) as lexical collisions accumulate (companion evaluation, §7.1.1 comparability note) — a measured instance of flat lexical retrieval degrading with corpus size.

## **3. The Space-Time Graph Data Model**

- **3.1 Design Principle: Three Irreducible Dimensions** - Knowledge bases have three orthogonal dimensions that no single 2D projection can faithfully represent: **time** (when was this written? how stale is it?), **ontology** (what is it about?), and **structure** (where does this paragraph sit within its document's hierarchy?). OHARA makes all three first-class by encoding them into a multi-modal graph stored in ArangoDB with a one-to-one mapping to the 3D sunburst-tunnel visualization.
- **3.2 Collections** - `documents` (root: source, title, entity_slugs, sumo_tags, published_date, decay_class, effective_decay_class, similar_to_indegree, description, structure_needs_review), `sections` (structural: level, title, summary), `paragraphs` (content: body, sumo_tags, entity_slugs, entity_types), `tables` (2D matrix), `entities` (canonical name, type, aliases, description, document_ids, mention_count), `edges` (single collection, typed by `relation` field).
- **3.3 Edge Taxonomy** - 7 typed edges partitioned into three categories: (a) Structural: `HAS_CHILD` (parent→child), `NEXT_SIBLING` (sequential ordering), `BELONGS_TO` (paragraph→document); (b) Semantic: `MENTIONS` (paragraph→entity), `RELATED_TO` (entity↔entity co-occurrence); (c) Cross-document: `SIMILAR_TO` (Jaccard-gated, LLM-enriched with verb/tags/summary/temporal_relation), `TOC_REF` (document→section resolved TOC entry). The key insight: `SIMILAR_TO` edges carry *narrative* metadata (`edge_verb`: "extends the argument of", `edge_summary`, `temporal_relation`: extends/supersedes/discusses) - not just a numeric weight. This is what makes cross-document traversal *explainable*, not just ranked.
- **3.4 Temporal Model** - Four decay classes with exponential decay rates: EVERGREEN (λ=10⁻⁶, ~infinite half-life - laws, math, classics), SCHOLARLY (λ=10⁻⁴, ~20-year half-life - papers, textbooks), CURRENT (λ=10⁻², ~70-day half-life - news, blogs), EPHEMERAL (λ=10⁻¹, ~7-day half-life - social posts, changelogs). Auto-promotion: `similar_to_indegree ≥ OHARA_SIMILAR_TO_EVERGREEN_THRESHOLD (5)` → `effective_decay_class = EVERGREEN` (often-cited documents are timeless regardless of declared class). Temporal scoring: `OHARA_TEMPORAL_WEIGHT × e^(−λ × Δt)` with a five-layer protection scheme (no temporal intent → skip; Principal tier immune; high-BM25 immune; decay only for weak candidates; temporal-coverage overlap boost for date-range queries). We present decay *scoring* as a guard-railed recency prior, not a measured ranking gain: on the one temporal benchmark evaluated (MultiHop-RAG, whose temporal queries test event ordering rather than recency) it is neutral-to-slightly-negative and the guards bound its effect to ~1pp (companion evaluation). The decay class's measured role is the visualization (temporal-reach auras, §6.5) and the Z-axis layout.
- **3.5 Formal Definition** - G = (V, E, τ, δ, σ) where V = ∪Cᵢ (C ∈ {documents, sections, paragraphs, tables, entities}), E ⊆ V × V × R (R = 7 relation types), τ: V_docs → Θ (temporal bucket function at configurable resolution), δ: V_docs → D (decay class ∈ {EVERGREEN, SCHOLARLY, CURRENT, EPHEMERAL}), σ: E_SIMILAR_TO → {verb, tags, summary, temporal_relation} (narrative enrichment). The graph supports O(|D|) pre-filtering at query time via document-rollup of entity_slugs and sumo_tags - a factor-of-chunks_per_doc reduction (~50–60× at our corpus densities) that flat RAG cannot achieve.

**Complexity of pre-filtering** (full derivation in the companion paper, §3.5): document rollup unions each document's `entity_slugs` and `sumo_tags` onto its root node at ingest, so query-time candidate pruning scans $O(n)$ document rollups ($n = |V_{docs}|$) instead of $O(n \times \text{chunks\_per\_doc})$ content nodes; any document whose rollup does not intersect the query's SUMO/entity fingerprint is skipped before structural traversal. Whether the subsequent *descent* finds the right paragraph is the empirical bottleneck — measured, document location-and-descent accounts for ~42% of corpus-wide retrieval failures (companion evaluation, §7.1.1) — so the pre-filter's cheapness is established while its sufficiency is not.

**Navigability property**: every content node is reachable from its document root in at most $L + 1$ `HAS_CHILD` hops, where $L \le 5$ is the section-hierarchy depth (document → L1 section → … → deepest section → paragraph/table), and every content node reaches its document root in exactly 1 hop via `BELONGS_TO`. Cross-document movement is always a short typed bridge: one `SIMILAR_TO` hop at document level, or two `MENTIONS` hops through a shared entity at paragraph level. Consequently any two content nodes in the corpus are connected by a path of at most $1 + 1 + (L{+}1) \le 8$ typed hops (paragraph → root → similar root → paragraph) with human-readable relation semantics at every step — the structural guarantee behind both the tier system's provenance trails and the visualization's lazy neighbor expansion.

## **4. Multi-Phase Hybrid Retrieval Engine**

- **4.1 Overview: From Flat List to Navigable Result** - OHARA's retrieval does not just rank chunks - it *navigates* a Space-Time Graph, crossing structural, semantic, temporal, and cross-document dimensions. Eight signal phases run in a configurable pipeline; results carry provenance for explainability. Measured honestly (companion evaluation, two corpora): the tuned pipeline ranks at *parity* with the best single dense signal — the differentiated wins are per-result provenance and corroboration-gated abstention, not ranking gains.
- **4.2 Phase 0 - Input Parsing & Query Fingerprinting** - Tokenize → classify keyword (≤3 tokens) / phrase (4–30) / paragraph (>30). For phrase+: Gemini extracts `{sumo_tags, entity_hints, temporal_intent}` (cached, temperature 0). Conversational RAG: last N Q&A turns prepended for anaphora resolution. This is *Adaptive RAG* - shallow retrieval for simple queries, deep fingerprinting for complex ones.
- **4.3 Phase 0b - TOC-Guided Section Selection (PageIndex-inspired)** - For phrase/paragraph queries, fetch top-3 seed document section trees (titles + LLM-generated summaries, no content) → ask Gemini which sections are relevant → use as structural traversal entry points. This directly addresses the PageIndex critique: OHARA borrows PageIndex's within-document precision but applies it as a *phase* within a multi-phase pipeline, not as the entire strategy.
- **4.4 Phase 1 - BM25 + SUMO Expansion** - ArangoSearch BM25 over content/title/markdown. Phase 1b: SUMO tag overlap + entity-type affinity. Hierarchy expansion up to `SUMO_HIERARCHY_DEPTH` ancestors. This is *Hybrid RAG* - keyword + ontology-semantic fused.
- **4.5 Phase 1c - Cross-Document Edge Expansion** - Multi-hop `SIMILAR_TO` traversal (graph `1..expandDepth ANY`). Results carry `edge_verb`, `edge_summary`, `hops`. This is *Multi-Hop RAG* - but with narrative edges, not latent-hops through embedding space. Narratively-enriched edges mean the user can *read why* two documents are connected, not just see a proximity score. Measured caveat: on MultiHop-RAG queries built to require multi-document evidence, ablating this phase is ranking-neutral — dense and lexical phases already surface the gold documents — so the supported claim is **explainable provenance paths**, not recall recovery.
- **4.5b Phase 1d - Vector Similarity** - Query embedded with `gemini-embedding-2` (768-d, cached); ArangoDB `COSINE_SIMILARITY` over paragraph embeddings. Measured as the **strongest single signal on both evaluation corpora**; the fusion pipeline reaches parity with it after weight tuning while adding tiers and provenance. Degrades gracefully when embeddings are absent.
- **4.6 Phase 2 - Entity Pivot** - Shared entity slugs across documents, damped by weight. `entity_types` array enables type-affinity scoring without extra AQL joins. This is *Memory-Augmented RAG* - persistent entity graph across all ingested docs.
- **4.7 Phase 3 - Structural Traversal + Corrective RAG** - AQL graph outbound from top node via structural edges (depth 2). Corrective RAG: zero-SUMO-overlap structural nodes dropped (TOC-guided nodes exempt). Structural proximity ≠ semantic relevance - a corrective filter that PageIndex lacks (PageIndex trusts tree structure implicitly).
- **4.8 Phase 4 - Score Fusion & Temporal Scoring** - Per-phase max-normalization, then weighted sum: BM25×0.6 + vector×1.0 + SUMO×0.4 + entity×0.6 + cross-doc×0.4 + structural×0.3 (BM25/vector weights tuned by grid search on one corpus and validated to transfer to a second; the original lexical-first default BM25×1.0 under-served semantic signal on both). Temporal decay added per node when `temporal_intent ≠ 'none'`, with five protection layers. This is *Context-Aware RAG* - temporal intent gates temporal scoring. Normalization is load-bearing: without it, raw BM25 magnitudes (≈13–24) silently reduce the multi-phase architecture to lexical search (companion evaluation, calibration findings).
- **4.9 Tier Classification - Berrypicking Meets Foraging Theory** - Inspired by Bates' Berrypicking (incremental info-seeking) and Pirolli's Information Foraging (follow scent, stop when weak): **Principal** (≥2 phases, cross-doc or multi-doc, score ≥ 75th pctl, capped at 5), **Integrity** (Principal + verified neighbours with provenance trail + optional LLM cross-check), **Explorer** (frontier one hop beyond Integrity, metadata-only, `stopped_reason` when scent weakens). This is *Citation-Aware RAG* + *Hierarchical RAG* + *Context-Ranking RAG* in one unified tier system. Two measured honesty notes (companion evaluation): (i) corroboration in the current architecture is **two-signal** (`bm25∩vector`) — the graph phases exclude already-seen nodes by design, so they expand the frontier rather than corroborate the core; (ii) the constraint's quantitative payoff is **abstention**: with identical retrieval, the ≥2-signal gate abstains on 45.6% of unanswerable queries versus 0.0% for a plain top-k tier, while holding 91.5% Principal-hit on answerable ones (figures at default weights; the tuned setting trades abstention down to 31.2% for ranking parity) — a top-k cut structurally cannot abstain.
- **4.10 Advanced Modes as RAG Pattern Orthography** - Table showing how OHARA subsumes 20+ RAG types as configuration toggles: Self-RAG (Principal responsiveness filter), Reasoning RAG (sub-query gap fill), Speculative RAG (pre-warm frontier), REFEED RAG (thumbs up/down → weight tuning), Agentic RAG (Gemini-driven tool dispatch loop), Chain-of-Retrieval (iterative Explorer-chasing). Each is a toggle, not a separate system. This architectural economy is itself a contribution - one engine, many behaviors.

**Status**: fusion + tier pseudocode and full per-phase ablations (two corpora) are in the companion evaluation paper (§4.10.1, §4.13 there); measured summary: every single-phase ablation lands within ±0.5pp of the full pipeline — ranking is carried by the dense+lexical pair, graph phases carry provenance. **Open (→ H4, Section 8.5)**: repeat the ablation matrix across corpus sizes (10 / 100 / 1,000 docs) to test whether graph phases gain ranking weight as corpora grow.

## **5. Ingest Pipeline: From Documents to Space-Time Graph**

- **5.1 Parsing & Dedup** - LiteParse (PDF/EPUB/DOCX) → Markdown; `.md` pass-through. SHA-256 dedup (skip if seen, override with `--force`). Web crawl: all pages from same hostname bundled into single document (one `documents` node per domain).
- **5.2 LLM Structuring - DoCO Typing** - Gemini `gemini-2.5-flash-lite` maps Markdown chunks to DoCO nodes (Chapter, Section, Paragraph, Table, Figure, Authors, Bibliography). Content-hash caching. Parallel batches (concurrency=4). Output schema includes per-node `sumo_candidate_tags`, `candidate_entities`, and document-level temporal fields (`published_date`, `temporal_coverage`, `temporal_granularity`, `temporal_confidence`, `decay_class`).
- **5.3 SUMO Tag Validation - Ontology Grounding** - Three-stage resolution against 22,700-entry SUMO index: exact → case/separator-insensitive → alias table (~50 LLM-emitted terms → canonical). Invalid tags dropped + logged. This is a precision gate that prevents hallucinated ontology mappings - a problem LLM Wiki faces directly (it relies on emergent clusters, not grounded ontology).
- **5.4 Entity Extraction & Canonical Dedup** - 8 entity types (PERSON, ORG, LOCATION, DATE, TECH, AMOUNT, EVENT, CONCEPT). Canonical dedup within node. `isOpaqueToken()` heuristic rejects machine-generated identifiers (hashes, UUIDs, base58) - domain-agnostic noise filter. Cross-document dedup (`entity_dedup.js`) merges entity nodes with matching `norm_key`, repoints all `MENTIONS` edges.
- **5.5 Collection Transform** - Normalize into 4 collections. Artifact filter: remove separator lines, TOC noise, short nodes. Fragment reattachment: merge orphaned short paragraphs into parent section. This is *Sparse RAG* at ingest time - the problem is solved structurally, not at retrieval time.
- **5.6 Edge Creation & Structural Verification** - Structural edges (`HAS_CHILD`, `NEXT_SIBLING`, `BELONGS_TO`) + semantic edges (`MENTIONS`, `RELATED_TO`). Document rollup: union entity_slugs + sumo_tags onto document node → O(docs) pre-filtering. `structure_needs_review: true` if level jumps > 1 detected - the PageIndex-inspired verification gate.
- **5.7 Cross-Document Similarity & Narrative Enrichment** - Jaccard on entity sets ≥ threshold → `SIMILAR_TO` edge. Gemini generates per-edge: `verb` (e.g. "extends the argument of"), `tags` (1–4 SUMO-style concepts), `summary` (≤60 words), `temporal_relation` (extends/supersedes/discusses, derived from verb at zero extra LLM cost). EVERGREEN auto-promotion: `similar_to_indegree ≥ 5` → `effective_decay_class = EVERGREEN`. This narrative enrichment means every cross-document edge is *human-readable* - the graph can be traversed by a reader, not just an algorithm.

**Measured cost (companion evaluation, §5 there)**: 200 QASPER academic papers = 6.2M prompt + 4.5M output tokens (~53k tokens/doc), **$2.41** at standard `gemini-2.5-flash-lite` rates (~$1.20 flex tier); 609 MultiHop-RAG news articles ≈ 19.5M tokens, **≈$4.50** — roughly **$12 per 1,000 documents** for the full semantic graph (DoCO structure, SUMO tags, entities, narrative cross-doc edges). Content-hash caching makes transient-failure recovery near-free (14.5% first-pass API failures on QASPER, all recovered by idempotent re-ingest at ~zero marginal tokens). Known caveat: ingest idempotency holds at chunk level, not edge level — forced re-ingest of partial documents duplicated structural edges, requiring a post-hoc dedup pass. **Open (→ H4, Section 8.5)**: entity dedup reduction ratio; head-to-head GraphRAG entity-extraction cost at the same corpus size.

## **6. The Space-Time Graph Visualization: A Tunnel Through Knowledge**

- **6.1 Why 3D? The Dimension Argument** - A knowledge base has three irreducible dimensions: time (publication date, temporal coverage), ontology (what the document is about), and structure (document hierarchy). Any 2D projection collapses at least one. OHARA's sunburst-tunnel layout preserves all three: **Z-axis = time, XY cross-section = ontology sunburst, local disc geometry = structure**. As data explodes, documents fill more disc positions along the tunnel and stack into topic columns - the visualization *benefits* from scale, unlike 2D node-link diagrams that become hairballs beyond ~100 nodes.
- **6.2 Sunburst-Tunnel Layout** - Documents mapped to Z via temporal bucketing at configurable resolution (day/week/month/year/decade/century). The XY cross-section is an **ontology sunburst plane**: each document sits at the polar coordinate of its top SUMO category (θ = category slice midpoint from a precomputed recursive angular partition of the SUMO tree; r = R_max·√(depth/D_max), abstract categories near the spine, specific toward the rim). Because the layout depends only on the ontology, not the corpus, it is stable across sessions, and documents sharing a category stack into fixed **topic columns** along Z - a reader follows one subject through time by looking down a single column. The user "flies through" the tunnel: close = recent, far = historical. This is *time as space*, extended into a volumetric graph where each time-slice carries the ontology map of its documents.
- **6.3 Radial Disc Structure - Document as Spine** - Each document occupies a circular disc perpendicular to Z at its temporal position, centered on its sunburst coordinate. Document node at center. Section nodes arranged in concentric rings by level (L1 closest → L5 outermost). Paragraph/table nodes on the outermost ring. Ring radius adapts dynamically: `max(35, ceil(n × (diam + gap) / 2π))`. This directly encodes the PageIndex-style tree hierarchy as a *spatial* structure - the user can see "this is a deep document" (many rings) vs. "this is flat" (few rings).
- **6.4 Entity & Tag Placement** - Named entities are placed at the centroid of the documents that mention them, pushed outward from the document cluster by a fixed offset (max disc radius + 120): entities spanning multiple documents float *between* them, single-document entities hover near their disc. Top-25 SUMO tags sit near the centroid of the sections whose paragraphs carry them, with deterministic hash-based jitter. Tether lines connect entities to their document discs, tags to their sections. This is the cross-document semantic layer: an entity shared by 5 documents produces 5 tether lines converging on one point between them - visual proof of cross-document connectivity that PageIndex and flat RAG simply cannot show.
- **6.5 Decay Auras - Time Made Visible** - Semi-transparent ellipsoids hugging each document disc and stretching along the Z-axis by decay class: EVERGREEN spans 5 disc-lengths, SCHOLARLY 2.5, CURRENT 1, EPHEMERAL 0.4 (pulsing opacity); per-instance coloring from the document's SUMO category, one `InstancedMesh` per class. These auras make *temporal reach* visually immediate: a user sees at a glance that a 1920s mathematics paper (EVERGREEN) still "reaches" into present-day Z positions, while a 2024 news article (EPHEMERAL) barely extends beyond its own disc. No other RAG system makes temporal decay *spatially* legible. (Note: decay's measured role is exactly this visual one - as a retrieval ranking signal it is neutral-to-negative on event-ordering benchmarks; see §3.4 and the companion evaluation.)
- **6.6 Rendering Architecture - Efficiency at Scale** - Three.js `InstancedMesh`: 1 draw call per shape bucket (sphere=doc, tetrahedron=section, box=paragraph, octahedron=table). Edges: single `LineSegments`. Decay auras: 4 `InstancedMesh` (one per class). Glow textures: cached per hex color. Labels: HTML overlay projected from 3D→2D per frame. **Lazy loading**: nodes fetched per-document on selection; neighbors fetched per-node on click. This means the visualization complexity is O(selected_docs), not O(total_corpus) - the user progressively loads only what they explore. This is the key to scaling: rendering cost is bounded by the working set, not the corpus. Measured (companion evaluation, §7.4 there): scene rebuild time and JS heap grow **sub-linearly** in node count (17× more nodes → ~1.7× rebuild, ~1.5× heap, 713→12,323 nodes), confirming per-node overhead is marginal; frame rate does decline with fill-rate under software GL (7→3 FPS, a conservative lower bound), so the honest ceiling is visual clutter and fill-rate, not geometry submission.
- **6.7 Color Modes as Analytical Lenses** - **by doc**: color from top SUMO category (golden-ratio HSL) → reveals ontology clusters at a glance. **by type**: fixed palette per node type/level → reveals structural patterns. **by SUMO**: per-tag hash → reveals semantic diversity within a document. Each mode is a different "mental spotlight" on the same graph - the user toggles lenses without re-fetching data.
- **6.8 Interaction Design** - Click node → select + expand lazy-loaded neighbors + render selection edges (highlighted graph context). Click disc plane → open section navigation panel. Raycaster for hover tooltips. OrbitControls with tunnel-entrance camera position. Zoom (+/-/0 keyboard). Drag guard (distinguish click from orbit). Query results cross-link: clicking a result card auto-selects its document and pulses the node.
- **6.9 How This Addresses the Scale Problem** - Summary: data explosion makes flat lists un-navigable and 2D graphs into hairballs. The sunburst-tunnel turns scale into an asset: more documents = denser topic columns and a more informative temporal spine. Structural rings make within-document navigation immediate. Centroid entity placement makes cross-document entity sharing visible. Decay auras make temporal reach spatial. The user navigates *space, topic, and time* instead of scrolling a list.

## **7. Evaluation**

The full evaluation lives in the companion paper ("Efficient Visualization of Knowledge Bases via Space-Time Graphs"); this section summarizes its measured findings as they bear on the architectural claims of §2–§6, and states what remains hypothesis.

- **7.1 Retrieval Quality (measured, two corpora)** - QASPER (200 papers, 150 questions, paragraph-level gold) and MultiHop-RAG (609 news articles, 500 stratified queries, document-level gold), full config matrix (BM25-only / vector-only / full / per-phase ablations — four on QASPER, five on MultiHop / tuned weights). Headline: the tuned full pipeline (BM25 0.6, vector 1.0) ranks at **parity with vector-only** on both corpora (QASPER Hits@10 33.3%, MRR 0.179 ≥ 0.175; MultiHop Hits@10 98.4%, MRR 0.812 ≥ 0.811) while emitting tiered, provenance-carrying results; every single-phase ablation is within ±0.5pp. The original hypothesis — multi-phase fusion *outranks* single signals as corpora grow — is **not supported at these scales**; the corpus-size sweep (10/100/1,000) needed to test the scaling form of the claim remains open, as do head-to-head GraphRAG-degradation measurements (entity saturation is argued in §2.2, not yet measured).
- **7.2 Cross-Document Reasoning (measured, reframed)** - On MultiHop-RAG queries constructed to require 2+ documents, ablating `SIMILAR_TO` expansion is ranking-neutral: dense+lexical signals already surface the gold documents. The measured cross-document differentiator is **provenance traceability** (narrative edges a user can read) and **corroboration-gated abstention** on unanswerable queries (45.6% vs. 0.0% top-k, at 91.5% Principal-hit) — not recall. PageIndex-style single-doc systems cannot express either; that comparison stands, but as an explainability claim.
- **7.3 Temporal Scoring Ablation (measured, honest negative)** - Removing decay *improves* the temporal-query slice (MRR 0.749 vs. 0.736) on MultiHop-RAG, whose temporal queries test event ordering, not recency; the five-layer guards bound the damage to ~1pp. Recency-semantic corpora remain the untested intended domain.
- **7.4 Visualization Task Performance (prepared, pending)** - Within-subjects protocol (n ≥ 8, counterbalanced, T1 temporal filtering / T2 topic tracing / T3 structural lookup, time + error + SUS), matched task sets with corpus-verified gold answers, and the flat-list comparison interface are built; the study awaits participants.
- **7.5 Rendering Scalability (measured)** - 713 → 12,323 nodes: scene rebuild 1.5 s → 2.3 s, JS heap 52 → 80 MB — sub-linear in node count, confirming InstancedMesh batching; idle FPS 7 → 3 under software GL (conservative lower bound). The honest scaling ceiling is fill-rate and visual clutter, not geometry submission.
- **7.6 End-to-End Generation (measured)** - 100-query MultiHop subsample, flash-lite generation from top-10 context: tuned full 54% vs. vector-only 57% — generation parity mirrors retrieval parity, both within the published retrieved-context band (0.44–0.56). Dominant failure is paragraph selection (41% "insufficient information"), not hallucination.
- **7.7 REFEED RAG Feedback Loop (open)** - Accuracy-by-rank improvement across feedback iterations; the grid-searched weight tuning above is the offline stand-in until human feedback accumulates.

## **8. Discussion**

- **8.1 When PageIndex Is Still Better** - For single-document QA on well-structured PDFs (financial reports, legal contracts), PageIndex's dedicated TOC-correction pipeline may outperform OHARA's more general approach. Acknowledge this: OHARA is designed for *multi-document, multi-temporal* corpora, not single-doc precision benchmarks.
- **8.2 What OHARA Can Learn from LLM Wiki** - Louvain community detection on `SIMILAR_TO` graph for emergent (not just top-down SUMO) clusters. "Surprising connections" - flagging unexpected cross-community edges. Adamic-Adar weighting for Phase 1c cross-doc edges. Two-step ingest (analyze contradictions before writing nodes). Web-augmented deep research tool for Agentic RAG when local corpus is insufficient.
- **8.3 Limitations** - Single LLM backend (Gemini); 3D visualization requires WebGL and may be disorienting for first-time users (mitigated in the study protocol by a warm-up task); no user study run yet (protocol, task sheets, and comparison interface are prepared); web crawl ingest per-hostname bundling may obscure important page-level distinctions; entity dedup is batch-only (not streaming); embedding coverage must be maintained per corpus (ingest-time embeddings required a model-tagged backfill pass in practice).
- **8.4 Threats to Validity** - Two clean born-digital corpora (academic papers, news) cannot separate genre from corpus effects; noisy OCR untested. Fusion weights tuned on one corpus (transferred unchanged to the second, but two corpora ≠ domain-general defaults). Doc-level vs. paragraph-level gold protocols are not cross-comparable. Gemini-specific prompts may not transfer to other LLMs. Visualization study, when run, must report per-participant learning effects.
- **8.5 Open Research Agenda** - Inherited from the companion evaluation (§8.4 there): (H1) many-angle corroboration — let graph phases re-score seen nodes so every phase becomes a ranking vote; (H2) recency-semantic temporal corpora (TempRAGEval-style) where the decay prior can be fairly tested; (H3) the human navigation benefit of the spatial-temporal mental model — the thesis claim, and the highest-leverage open item. This paper's scale claims (§2's entity-saturation and flat-list-degradation arguments; §7.1's corpus-size sweep) join that agenda as (H4): measured scaling curves against GraphRAG and flat RAG at 10/100/1,000+ documents.

## **9. Conclusion**

- The Space-Time Graph is a data model and visualization metaphor designed for the regime where data outgrows flat lists and 2D graphs. By encoding time as a spatial axis (tunnel Z), ontology as a stable sunburst cross-section, and structure as local disc geometry, OHARA turns corpus growth from a retrieval liability into a navigable landscape. The companion evaluation grounds the architecture honestly: multi-phase retrieval delivers tiered provenance and corroboration-gated abstention *at ranking parity* with the best single dense signal — a knowledge base that is **seeable and auditable**, not one that outranks dense retrieval. SUMO-validated ontology tags prevent hallucinated semantics and anchor the visualization's coordinate system. Decay auras make temporal reach spatially legible even where decay scoring does not help ranking. Ingest builds the full semantic graph at ~$12 per 1,000 documents; rendering scales sub-linearly. The system subsumes 20+ RAG patterns as configuration toggles rather than separate architectures — an architectural economy claim this paper argues and the open agenda (§8.5) must stress-test at scale. Future work: many-angle corroboration, recency-semantic temporal benchmarks, the user study, corpus-size scaling curves, Louvain emergent clusters, multi-LLM backend support.
